Campfire Rules
🔥
Fire Restrictions — Check Before You Go
July 4th weekend often brings Stage 1 or Stage 2 fire restrictions to Prescott National Forest. Confirm current restrictions at fs.usda.gov or call 928-443-8000. Fires are only permitted in the designated steel ring or concrete grill on our site — never on the ground.
  • Keep fire within the designated fire ring — never on the ground
  • Bucket of water nearby at all times when fire is lit
  • Drown, stir, feel — fire is out when ash is cold to touch
  • No fireworks at the campsite — follow campground rules
  • Pack out all ash and fire debris if required by campground
  • Check quiet hours — usually 10 PM, respect your neighbors

Cooking Setup
🍳 Blackstone & Fire Pit
  • Blackstone camping grill — propane tank + extra canister
  • Blackstone scraper / spatulas / tongs
  • Blackstone seasoning oil (or cooking spray)
  • Fire pit — check campground allows personal fire pits (site has a steel ring; yours may need to stay in the car)
  • Firewood — buy locally in Prescott (don't transport across regions)
  • Firestarter / matches / lighter
  • Camp chairs (enough for everyone) + folding table
  • Plates, cups, utensils — paper/plastic or camping set
  • Pot + pan if doing eggs / anything not on the Blackstone
  • Dish soap, sponge, small wash basin
  • Aluminum foil — endless uses at camp
  • S'more sticks / roasting skewers
  • Trash bags — pack it in, pack it out
Cooler & Supplies
🧊 Food & Essentials
  • 2 coolers: one for food, one for drinks (food stays colder)
  • Twice as much ice as you think — 91°F days in July
  • Freeze water bottles to use as ice packs → drinking water later
  • Paper towels + napkins
  • Ziploc bags (all sizes)
  • Headlamps / flashlights for everyone (arriving Friday night in the dark!)
  • Lantern for the canopy / table area
  • First aid kit + any medications
  • Sunscreen — UV is stronger at 5,641 ft
  • Bug spray — July evenings can be buggy
  • Portable battery pack / phone chargers
  • Kids' activities: cards, frisbee, glow sticks for fireworks night
  • Light layers for the evenings (lows in the mid-60s)

Pack Smart
Cooler Strategy
  • Pre-form burger patties and wrap individually — easier at camp
  • Pre-crack eggs into a sealed container — skip the shells at camp
  • Bring twice the ice you think you need — it melts fast in July
  • Keep a separate small cooler for drinks so the food cooler stays cold
  • Freeze water bottles: they become ice packs, then drinking water
  • Ziplock bags for everything — condensation is real
  • Pack food in reverse meal order — Sunday stuff goes in last, comes out first
