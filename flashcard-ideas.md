## Already in the App (12 cards)
1. What is the capital of Alaska? → Juneau (sea/air only)
2. Why is glacier ice blue? → absorbs all light except blue
3. What is "calving"? → glacier chunks crashing into the sea
4. How many bald eagles in Alaska? → 30,000+
5. What does "Ketchikan" mean? → "thundering wings of an eagle" (Tlingit)
6. What is a "raft" of sea otters? → holding paws so they don't drift
7. What year did Alaska become a US state? → 1959, 49th state
8. Why did people rush to Skagway in 1898? → Klondike Gold Rush
9. What do totem poles tell? → family histories and clan stories
10. What is the Inside Passage? → protected coastal water route
11. How deep can a harbor seal dive? → 1,600 feet, 28 minutes breath-hold
12. How fast does Mendenhall Glacier retreat? → 100–150 feet per year

## Ideas to Add

### Geography
- How big is Alaska compared to other states? → Largest US state, twice the size of Texas
- How long is Alaska's coastline? → 33,000+ miles — more than all other US states combined
- What are the five boroughs of— wait, wrong state 😄
- How much daylight in Juneau in June? → Up to 18 hours — sun sets around 10 PM
- What mountain range is near Skagway? → Coast Mountains, with White Pass at 2,885 ft

### Wildlife
- What do brown bears eat besides salmon? → Berries, roots, insects, small mammals
- How do orcas hunt? → Work together in pods like wolves — cooperative hunting
- What makes a puffin special at fishing? → Can carry 10–12 small fish crosswise in its beak
- How long do humpback whales live? → 80–90 years
- Why do salmon return to where they were born? → Navigation by Earth's magnetic field + smell of their home stream

### History & Culture
- Who sold Alaska to the US and for how much? → Russia, $7.2 million in 1867 ($140M today)
- What was Alaska called when it was purchased? → "Seward's Folly" — many thought it was a bad deal
- Who was "Soapy Smith"? → Skagway's most famous con man, ran scams on Gold Rush miners
- What were the two routes prospectors took to the Yukon? → White Pass Trail and Chilkoot Trail
- What Native peoples live in Southeast Alaska? → Tlingit, Haida, and Tsimshian

### Glaciers
- How long is Tracy Arm Fjord? → About 30 miles of narrow, glacier-carved water
- What is a "fjord"? → A narrow sea inlet carved by a glacier thousands of years ago
- How old is the ice in a glacier? → Hundreds to thousands of years old
- What's the difference between a glacier and an iceberg? → Glacier is connected to land; iceberg is floating ice that calved off
- How thick can a glacier be? → Some Alaskan glaciers are over a mile thick

### Ship Life
- What is the Disney Wonder's capacity? → About 1,750 guests + 950 crew
- What deck is the pool on? → Deck 9 — Goofy's Pool for families
- What is Palo? → Adult-only restaurant on the ship with Italian cuisine
- How fast does the Disney Wonder travel? → About 21–24 knots (24–27 mph)
- What is the ship's registry? → The Bahamas (most cruise ships use flags of convenience)

### Kids' Favorites (for River age 9 and Sedona age 12)
- What is the biggest animal ever to live on Earth? → Blue whale (bigger than any dinosaur!)
- Can you eat glacier ice? → Yes! It's ancient frozen rain — totally clean
- Why don't fish freeze in icy Alaska waters? → Special proteins in their blood act like antifreeze
- How do bears know when to wake up from hibernation? → Longer daylight hours trigger hormonal changes
- What is bioluminescence? → Ocean creatures that glow — sometimes visible at night from the ship deck
