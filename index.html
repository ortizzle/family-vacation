History & Culture
⛏️
The Klondike Gold Rush (1896–1899)
Gold was discovered in Canada's Yukon Territory in 1896. Over 100,000 prospectors flooded through Skagway. Most found nothing. The White Pass route you're taking was the "easier" of two treacherous mountain trails.
🏛️
Tlingit, Haida & Tsimshian Peoples
Southeast Alaska has been home to Indigenous peoples for over 10,000 years. The totem poles in Ketchikan are Tlingit and Haida art — each tells a story about family, history, and the natural world, meant to be read top to bottom like a visual family history carved in cedar.
🧊
How Glaciers Form
Glaciers form when snow accumulates faster than it melts over hundreds of years. The weight compresses snow into dense blue-tinted ice. A tidewater glacier flows all the way to the ocean, where the ends "calve" in spectacular crashes. Mendenhall Glacier retreats about 100–150 feet per year.
🐟
The Salmon Cycle
Alaska's five salmon species are born in freshwater, migrate to the ocean, then return to the exact stream where they were born to spawn — and die. Their bodies feed the entire rainforest ecosystem. June is the start of salmon season in Ketchikan.
🌟
Alaska by the Numbers
Largest US state (twice the size of Texas). 3 million lakes. More coastline than all other US states combined. 17 of the 20 highest US peaks. In June, expect daylight until 10 PM or later — pack an eye mask.

About Dawes Glacier
Dawes is a tidewater glacier approximately 600 feet tall and about a mile wide — one of the most actively calving glaciers in Southeast Alaska. The fjord walls rise over 3,000 feet on both sides. The wider approach channel (vs. Tracy Arm's narrow sections) means the ship can often get closer to the face. Harbor seals and bald eagles are common; occasional humpbacks pass through. When ice calves off, the sound is like a cannon shot.

💡 Alaska Cruise Pro Tips
Tap to expand
🧅Layer everything. Temps swing 20°F+ from morning to afternoon. Base layer, fleece, waterproof shell — bring all three every port day.
☂️Rain gear is non-negotiable. Even Skagway (the driest port) can surprise you. Ketchikan averages 160 inches of rain per year — pack ponchos for the kids.
🌅Eye masks for sleeping. In June you'll have 19 hours of daylight. It won't get dark. Blackout curtains help, but an eye mask is your friend.
🔭Binoculars on deck at all times. Humpbacks, orcas, bald eagles — they appear when you least expect it. Keep them accessible, not in the stateroom.
🛳️Your verandah is gold. Alaska is one of the best routes for verandah use — scenic cruising, wildlife from bed, glacier day views. Use it constantly.
🗺️Watch the navigation screen. Deck 4 lobby has a live ship position map. Kids love watching it update as you move through the Inside Passage.
👟Waterproof shoes every port. Docks, gangways, and trails are wet. Leave the white sneakers for sea days.
🧲Bring magnetic hooks. Stateroom walls are magnetic — hooks let you hang wet layers and gear without cluttering the small bathroom.
👕Don't over-pack clothes. Laundry rooms are on board. Pack fewer outfits, wash mid-cruise, and leave room in your bag for Alaska souvenirs.

🌊 Journey Fun Facts
🗺️
The Inside Passage
Your entire Alaska route runs through the Inside Passage — a 1,000+ mile natural sheltered waterway protected from the open Pacific by thousands of islands. Ships have sailed it since the Klondike Gold Rush. Over 2 million people cruise it every year.
🧊
Endicott Arm & Dawes Glacier (2026)
Tracy Arm is closed for 2026 after an August 2025 landslide triggered a tsunami inside the fjord. Your glacier day visits Endicott Arm — home of Dawes Glacier, a 600-foot-tall, one-mile-wide tidewater glacier that calves ice so actively you can hear it from the ship's deck. Granite walls rise 3,000 feet on both sides. Harbor seals ride ice floes. Many cruisers consider it more dramatic than Tracy Arm.
⚓
Vancouver → Skagway
The one-way run from Vancouver to Skagway (the northernmost port) is roughly 800 nautical miles. The Wonder cruises at about 19 knots — that's 22 mph — to cover the distance across two sea days. The ship never once touches the open Pacific Ocean.
🏔️
Gold Rush Footsteps
In 1898, over 100,000 prospectors poured through Skagway to reach the Klondike goldfields in the Yukon. They took the same waterway your ship travels. The White Pass & Yukon Route railway you're riding was built in 1898 to carry them over the mountains — and it still runs today.
🌅
19 Hours of Daylight
In mid-June at these latitudes you get nearly 19 hours of daylight. The sun sets around 10:30 PM and rises before 4 AM. It never gets fully dark. This is why Alaska summer wildlife is so active — and why kids (and parents) need eye masks.
🐋
Wildlife Capital of the World
The Inside Passage hosts more humpback whales per mile than almost any waterway on Earth in summer. You'll also be in range of orca pods, Dall's porpoise, Steller sea lions, bald eagles (Alaska has over 30,000), brown bears, black bears, mountain goats, and Sitka black-tailed deer.
🛳️
Disney Wonder by the Numbers
Built in 1999, 85,000 gross tons, 964 feet long, 13 decks. She carries 2,713 passengers and 950 crew. Her signature horn plays "When You Wish Upon a Star." She's one of the smallest ships still doing full Alaska itineraries — which means she can sail deeper into Endicott Arm's narrow fjord than her larger competitors.

🏔️ Vancouver Fun Facts
🌲
Third-Largest City in Canada
Greater Vancouver has 2.6 million people, making it Canada's third-largest metro after Toronto and Montreal. Richmond — your home base — is a city of 230,000 within that metro, famous for having North America's most authentic Chinese food scene outside of China.
🎬
Hollywood North
Vancouver is one of the world's biggest film and TV production hubs — sometimes called "Hollywood North." The X-Files, Deadpool, Twilight, and dozens of Marvel films were shot here. You'll likely walk past an active film set without knowing it.
🌧️
Rain Capital of Canada
Vancouver is the rainiest major city in Canada, averaging 166 rainy days per year. June is the last of the wet months before the gorgeous dry summer. Locals wear rain jackets like a second skin and rarely use umbrellas — take note.
🏔️
Mountains Meet Ocean
Vancouver is one of the few cities where you can ski in the morning and kayak in the ocean in the afternoon. The North Shore mountains (where Capilano is) rise to 4,000+ feet just 20 minutes from downtown. On a clear day the view from the city includes snow-capped peaks.
⚽
FIFA World Cup 2026 Host
Vancouver's BC Place stadium is one of 16 host venues across USA, Canada, and Mexico for the 2026 FIFA World Cup — the largest World Cup in history with 48 teams. The group stage matches happening during your stay make you part of a once-in-a-generation event.
🍁 Vancouver & Canada Tips
Tap to expand
🕐No jet lag. Vancouver is Pacific Time — same as Phoenix in summer (both on PDT). Your body clock won't notice a thing.
💵Canadian dollars. 1 USD ≈ 1.40 CAD. Credit cards work everywhere. Bring ~$60 CAD cash for food courts (Parker Place) and Richmond Night Market stalls.
🚇Tap to ride the SkyTrain. No tickets needed — tap your Visa, Mastercard, or Amex directly on the fare gate. Kids under 13 ride free. Trains every 5–8 minutes.
🌧️Pack rain gear, especially June 13. Vancouver June averages 13 rainy days. June 13 (North Shore day) is forecast as the wettest — ponchos for the kids, waterproof jacket for adults.
🌡️Layer up. Expect 63–72°F days but cooler mornings and evenings. A light fleece or zip-up is perfect after 7 PM, especially on the North Shore.
📱US phones work in Canada but most carriers charge roaming. Check your plan — many include Canada (T-Mobile does by default). Turn on "international data" before landing.
✈️Customs at YVR. Fill out the CBSA declaration on your phone before landing (paper forms available too). Be honest — fruit, meat, and large amounts of cash must be declared. Standard tourist entry, no visa needed for US citizens.
🍽️Tipping is the same as the US. 15–20% at restaurants, $2–3 per taxi or rideshare ride. Some Richmond food court stalls have tip prompts — 0% is totally fine there.
🏙️Richmond vs Vancouver. Your hotel is in Richmond (suburb, 30 min south of downtown). Richmond has its own amazing food scene. "Going to Vancouver" means taking the Canada Line north — ~30 min, $6.60 CAD (~$4.70 USD) one-way per person.
🆘Emergency is still 911. Same number as the US. BC Health care is excellent. The nearest urgent care to River Rock is Richmond Hospital (~10 min drive).

Did You Know?
Disney Wonder is only the second Disney cruise ship ever built. Her horn plays "When You Wish Upon a Star." The Mickey ears on the funnel are a signature of every Disney ship — and the classic navy-and-yellow hull is a nod to the golden age of ocean liners.
Alaska Cruise Tips
🧊Glacier Day (Day 3): Head to the bow or upper-forward decks early. The ship gets close enough to hear calving — the crack echoes off canyon walls. Layer up; it's 15–20°F colder near the ice.
🌅Midnight sun: In June, it stays light until 10–11 PM. Pack a sleep mask — the stateroom curtains don't block it all. Kids especially won't want to sleep.
📱Disney Navigator app: Download before you sail. Shows daily schedule, restaurant times, character meet locations, and your activity reservations — no cell service needed onboard.
🐋Wildlife deck watching: The Inside Passage is rich in orca, humpbacks, and Dall's porpoise. Early mornings (6–8 AM) and evenings at the bow are the best times. Bring binoculars.
🏴‍☠️Pirate Night: One evening becomes a full deck party with costumes, fireworks at sea, and a character deck show. Wear your pirate gear!
🎵Horn signals: 3 blasts = leaving port. The kids will love anticipating the departure horn in each Alaskan port.

What is the World Cup?
⚽The FIFA World Cup is the biggest sporting event on the planet. Nearly every country on Earth tries to qualify — only 48 make the cut for 2026. It happens every four years. The last one was 2022 in Qatar.
🏆FIFA stands for Fédération Internationale de Football Association — the organization that governs global soccer. Think of them like the NFL, except every nation on Earth is a member.
🌎How it works: 48 teams are sorted into 12 groups of 4. Each team plays the other 3 in its group. The top 2 from each group — plus the 8 best 3rd-place teams — advance to a 32-team knockout bracket. One loss and you're done, all the way to the final.
🎉Why it matters: Players like Messi, Ronaldo, and Mbappé spend their entire careers chasing this trophy. Fans from every country travel the world to watch their team. In 2026, it comes to your backyard — and you'll be there for it.
You're There For It

🎉FIFA Fan Festival (vancouverfwc26.ca): The official fan event runs June 11–July 19 at PNE Grounds, Hastings Park in East Vancouver. Free general admission. Expect live match broadcasts on giant screens, live music (Canadian + global artists), family soccer games, obstacle courses, food vendors, and merchandise. Premium reserved amphitheatre seating is available for a fee. The festival opens the same day you arrive in Vancouver — any of your four pre-cruise days work.
The Tournament
🌎Format: 48 teams split into 12 groups of 4. Top 2 from each group plus 8 best third-place teams advance to a 32-team knockout round. More matches, more upsets, more drama.
🇺🇸USA host cities: New York, Los Angeles, Dallas, San Francisco, Seattle, Atlanta, Boston, Kansas City, Philadelphia, Miami, Guadalupe (Texas).
🇨🇦Canada host cities: Vancouver and Toronto — Canada's only two venues.
🏆Final: July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York area).
Kids Corner ⚽
🧒 Fun Facts
The 2026 World Cup has 48 teams — that's 16 more countries than ever before! Every continent is represented. Some teams playing in Vancouver may have never qualified for a World Cup before. You might be watching history. The atmosphere inside BC Place during a match is unlike anything else — 54,000 people from all over the world, all watching the same game.
🌍
Why Three Countries?
The 2026 World Cup bid — called "United 2026" — was a joint proposal by the USA, Canada, and Mexico. It won over Morocco's bid in 2018. Sharing the tournament across North America means shorter travel for US and Canadian fans and an enormous economic boost for all three countries.
🇲🇽
Mexico Makes History
Mexico becomes the first country to host matches at three separate World Cups (1970, 1986, and 2026). The opening match is at the legendary Estadio Azteca in Mexico City on June 11 — the same day your family arrives in Vancouver.
📺
On the Ship
The group stage runs right through your cruise. Two BC Place matches fall during your sailing — Canada vs. Qatar (Jun 18, Skagway day, 3pm) and New Zealand vs. Egypt (Jun 21, last sea day, 6pm). Disney Wonder's pub and lounges will almost certainly have screens on. Watching World Cup soccer on a cruise ship in Alaska sounds like a pretty great story.
