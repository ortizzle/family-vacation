History & Culture
⛏️
The Klondike Gold Rush (1896–1899)
Gold was discovered in Canada's Yukon Territory in 1896. Over 100,000 prospectors flooded through Skagway. Most found nothing. The White Pass route you're taking was the "easier" of two treacherous mountain trails.
🏛️
Tlingit, Haida & Tsimshian Peoples
Southeast Alaska has been home to Indigenous peoples for over 10,000 years. The totem poles in Ketchikan are Tlingit and Haida art — each tells a story about family, history, and the natural world, meant to be read top to bottom like a visual family history carved in cedar.
🧊
How Glaciers Form
Glaciers form when snow accumulates faster than it melts over hundreds of years. The weight compresses snow into dense blue-tinted ice. A tidewater glacier flows all the way to the ocean, where the ends "calve" in spectacular crashes. Mendenhall Glacier retreats about 100–150 feet per year.
🐟
The Salmon Cycle
Alaska's five salmon species are born in freshwater, migrate to the ocean, then return to the exact stream where they were born to spawn — and die. Their bodies feed the entire rainforest ecosystem. June is the start of salmon season in Ketchikan.
🌟
Alaska by the Numbers
Largest US state (twice the size of Texas). 3 million lakes. More coastline than all other US states combined. 17 of the 20 highest US peaks. In June, expect daylight until 10 PM or later — pack an eye mask.

💡 Alaska Cruise Pro Tips
Tap to expand
🧅Layer everything. Temps swing 20°F+ from morning to afternoon. Base layer, fleece, waterproof shell — bring all three every port day.
☂️Rain gear is non-negotiable. Even Skagway (the driest port) can surprise you. Ketchikan averages 160 inches of rain per year — pack ponchos for the kids.
🌅Eye masks for sleeping. In June you'll have 19 hours of daylight. It won't get dark. Blackout curtains help, but an eye mask is your friend.
🔭Binoculars on deck at all times. Humpbacks, orcas, bald eagles — they appear when you least expect it. Keep them accessible, not in the stateroom.
🛳️Your verandah is gold. Alaska is one of the best routes for verandah use — scenic cruising, wildlife from bed, glacier day views. Use it constantly.
🗺️Watch the navigation screen. Deck 4 lobby has a live ship position map. Kids love watching it update as you move through the Inside Passage.
👟Waterproof shoes every port. Docks, gangways, and trails are wet. Leave the white sneakers for sea days.
🧲Bring magnetic hooks. Stateroom walls are magnetic — hooks let you hang wet layers and gear without cluttering the small bathroom.
👕Don't over-pack clothes. Laundry rooms are on board. Pack fewer outfits, wash mid-cruise, and leave room in your bag for Alaska souvenirs.

Did You Know?
During the 1898 Gold Rush, over 100,000 prospectors flooded through Skagway. The White Pass & Yukon Route railway you're riding was built in just 26 months through some of the most rugged terrain in North America.
Top Highlights
🚂White Pass Scenic Railway ✅ Booked for all three families (8:00–10:45 AM — see Today's Schedule). The narrow-gauge train climbs 2,865 ft in 20 miles through sheer granite cliffs, hand-drilled tunnels, and suspension bridges. The same route 100,000 gold-rush prospectors struggled up on foot in 1898. Views are jaw-dropping — cameras out the whole way.
🐕Musher's Camp & Sled Dogs ✅ Booked for Ortiz + Stallings (1:30–5:00 PM — see Today's Schedule). Meet real Iditarod racing dogs, learn how mushers train and care for them, and take a cart ride through the forest. Kids can hold and pet the puppies. Consistently one of the most-loved excursions of the whole cruise.
🏛️Klondike Gold Rush NHP Free, self-guided, right downtown. Park rangers in period costume give talks about Soapy Smith, the Chilkoot Trail, and life in 1898 Skagway. The visitor center film (~30 min) is excellent context before walking Broadway Street. The buildings themselves are original Gold Rush era — maintained by the NPS. nps.gov/klgo →
🎭Days of '98 Show The longest-running show in Alaska (performed since 1927) in the historic Eagles Hall on Broadway. Melodrama, can-can dancers, and the real story of con man Soapy Smith vs. the town hero Frank Reid — a showdown that ended with both men dead. About 45 min, runs multiple times daily. Good for the gap between morning and afternoon excursions. days98show.com →
🚶Broadway Street Skagway's main street is a living Gold Rush museum — most buildings are original 1898 construction maintained by the NPS. Browse local art, gold nugget jewelry, and Alaska-made souvenirs. Street performers and ranger demonstrations pop up throughout the day. Worth 30–45 min of walking end to end.
⛏️Gold Panning Several spots along Broadway offer paid gold panning — real glacial sediment, and you keep what you find (usually small flakes, occasionally a nice nugget). The NPS visitor center sometimes hosts free demos. River and Sedona will absolutely love this.
🧒 Kids Corner
A con man named "Soapy Smith" ran scams in Skagway — tricking miners out of their gold using fake telegraph offices and rigged card games. He's now one of Alaska's most famous villains! Ask a park ranger about him when you visit.

Did You Know?
Juneau is the only U.S. state capital with no road access. It sits between two mountains and the Tongass National Forest. The Mendenhall Glacier retreats about 100–150 feet per year — it could disappear by 2050.
Top Highlights
✈️5-Glacier Seaplane ✅ Booked for the Ortiz family (10:30 AM–Noon — see Today's Schedule). A floatplane takes off from the Gastineau Channel and banks over five glaciers in the Juneau Icefield — the largest non-polar icefield in North America. The perspective from above is something you cannot get on the ground. River and Sedona will be stunned. Dress in warm layers — it's cold at altitude.
🧊Mendenhall Glacier ✅ Booked for the Stallings family (8:30 AM–Noon — see Today's Schedule). Only 12 miles from downtown, Mendenhall is the most accessible glacier in Alaska. The NPS visitor center sits right at the glacier face with views of the ice and Nugget Falls waterfall. In June there's a chance of ice caves. Pack layers — it's 15°F colder at the ice. Mendenhall NPS →
🚠Mt. Roberts Tramway A 6-minute gondola ride right from the cruise dock up to 1,800 ft elevation. Sweeping views of the Gastineau Channel, surrounding peaks, and the ship below. There's a nature center, an eagle sanctuary (live injured eagles), short hiking trails, and a restaurant at the top. ~$35/adult, $20/child. Great option for families with free afternoon time. goldbelttours.com →
🐋Alaska Whales & Science Adventure ✅ Booked for the Simmons family (9:00 AM–2:30 PM — see Today's Schedule). Juneau's waters are prime humpback habitat in June. For everyone else: watch from the ship as you enter and leave the channel — humpbacks often surface near the route. Keep binoculars ready.
🍺Alaskan Brewing Co. Alaska's most celebrated craft brewery, started in 1986 from a recipe found in a turn-of-the-century newspaper. Free tours and tastings. About 15 min from the dock (cab ~$15 each way). The Alaskan Amber is the flagship. Adults-only for tastings, but kids are welcome in the gift shop. Good option for adults with free time after morning excursions. alaskanbeer.com →
🌊Tracy Arm Fjord You cruised Tracy Arm the day before (Day 3). If you didn't make it on deck, Juneau sits near the same fjord system — the entire Inside Passage here is glacier-carved. Mendenhall and the seaplane route both show you the same geological story up close.
🧒 Kids Corner
The Mendenhall Glacier is retreating about 100–150 feet every single year. You're looking at something that might not exist when Sedona and River are grown up! Can you spot the blue ice? Blue = the oldest, most compressed ice — over 3,000 years old.

Did You Know?
Ketchikan is one of the rainiest cities in the US. Locals say: "If you can't see the mountains, it's raining. If you can see them, it's about to rain." The lush temperate rainforest it creates is spectacular in June.
Top Highlights
🪵Great Alaskan Lumberjack Show ✅ Booked for all three families (3:30–5:30 PM — see Today's Schedule). A competitive show featuring real logging skills: log rolling, springboard chopping, axe throwing, and 50-ft tree climbing. Teams represent the US and Canada. The outdoor stadium has covered seating — it runs rain or shine, and in Ketchikan it will rain. Kids can get close to the competitors after the show. lumberjackshow.com →
🪶Totem Heritage Center The world's largest collection of original, unrestored totem poles — 33 poles recovered from abandoned Tlingit and Haida villages in the 1970s. These aren't tourist reproductions; some are over 150 years old. Guides explain the stories carved into each pole (Eagle = nobility, Raven = transformation, Bear = strength). About $8/adult, $5/child. 10-min walk from the dock. Visit Ketchikan →
🛶Creek Street Ketchikan's most photographed spot — a row of colorful historic buildings on stilts built over Ketchikan Creek. In the early 1900s it was the city's red-light district; today it's boutique shops, galleries, and the Dolly's House Museum. Walk the boardwalk and look down into the creek below — salmon fight the current right beneath your feet in summer.
🐟Salmon in the Creek June marks the start of salmon season, and Ketchikan Creek runs right through downtown. Salmon surge upstream to spawn and you can watch them from the boardwalk — free, no excursion needed. Dozens of fish fighting the current inches below you. River and Sedona will absolutely lose their minds over this.
🌧️Pothole Pub A no-frills local bar that Ketchikan residents actually go to — a world away from the cruise-ship souvenir strip. Cash only, cheap drinks, and locals who will happily talk to visitors. Good adult option after the Lumberjack Show wraps up. Kids version: grab fresh fish and chips at one of the waterfront stands.
🦅Eagle Spotting Ketchikan has an abundance of bald eagles — look up along the waterfront, fishing piers, and trees near the creek. When salmon are running, eagles hunt right near the water. No excursion needed: just look up as you walk around town. Your best free wildlife moment of the whole trip, and it requires zero planning.
🧒 Kids Corner
Totem poles are like giant comic books carved in cedar! Each figure tells part of a story — a family's history, an important event, or a legend. Eagle = nobility, Raven = transformation, Bear = strength. Look for these at Totem Heritage Center — some of the poles here are over 150 years old!

Did You Know?
Disney Wonder is only the second Disney cruise ship ever built. Her horn plays "When You Wish Upon a Star." The Mickey ears on the funnel are a signature of every Disney ship — and the classic navy-and-yellow hull is a nod to the golden age of ocean liners.
Alaska Cruise Tips
🧊Glacier Day (Day 3): Head to the bow or upper-forward decks early. The ship gets close enough to hear calving — the crack echoes off canyon walls. Layer up; it's 15–20°F colder near the ice.
🌅Midnight sun: In June, it stays light until 10–11 PM. Pack a sleep mask — the stateroom curtains don't block it all. Kids especially won't want to sleep.
📱Disney Navigator app: Download before you sail. Shows daily schedule, restaurant times, character meet locations, and your activity reservations — no cell service needed onboard.
🐋Wildlife deck watching: The Inside Passage is rich in orca, humpbacks, and Dall's porpoise. Early mornings (6–8 AM) and evenings at the bow are the best times. Bring binoculars.
🏴‍☠️Pirate Night: One evening becomes a full deck party with costumes, fireworks at sea, and a character deck show. Wear your pirate gear!
🎵Horn signals: 3 blasts = leaving port. The kids will love anticipating the departure horn in each Alaskan port.

FIFA World Cup 2026
June 11 – July 19, 2026 · USA · Canada · Mexico
What is the World Cup?
⚽The FIFA World Cup is the biggest sporting event on the planet. Nearly every country on Earth tries to qualify — only 48 make the cut for 2026. It happens every four years. The last one was 2022 in Qatar.
🏆FIFA stands for Fédération Internationale de Football Association — the organization that governs global soccer. Think of them like the NFL, except every nation on Earth is a member.
🌎How it works: 48 teams are sorted into 12 groups of 4. Each team plays the other 3 in its group. The top 2 from each group — plus the 8 best 3rd-place teams — advance to a 32-team knockout bracket. One loss and you're done, all the way to the final.
🎉Why it matters: Players like Messi, Ronaldo, and Mbappé spend their entire careers chasing this trophy. Fans from every country travel the world to watch their team. In 2026, it comes to your backyard — and you'll be there for it.
You're There For It
The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first hosted across three countries with 48 teams — the largest ever. Vancouver is a host city and your pre-cruise stay (June 11–15) overlaps with the group stage.

Vancouver at BC Place
🏟️BC Place Stadium is Vancouver's domed stadium in the heart of downtown — about 20–25 min from River Rock Hotel in Richmond. It hosted the 2010 Winter Olympics closing ceremony and the 2015 Women's World Cup final.
📅Group stage matches at Vancouver start June 11. Check the official FIFA schedule at FIFA.com for the exact match days and kickoff times — Vancouver is hosting multiple group stage games.
🎟️Tickets: World Cup tickets are allocated through FIFA's official ballot system. Walk-up or resale tickets may be available near the stadium on match days — but expect high demand and prices for popular group matchups.
🚇Getting there: Canada Line SkyTrain from Richmond (near your hotel) drops you at Olympic Village or Yaletown — both short walks to BC Place. Avoid driving; parking near the stadium fills fast on match days.
🎉FIFA Fan Festival (vancouverfwc26.ca): The official fan event runs June 11–July 19 at PNE Grounds, Hastings Park in East Vancouver. Free general admission. Expect live match broadcasts on giant screens, live music (Canadian + global artists), family soccer games, obstacle courses, food vendors, and merchandise. Premium reserved amphitheatre seating is available for a fee. The festival opens the same day you arrive in Vancouver — any of your four pre-cruise days work.
The Tournament
🌎Format: 48 teams split into 12 groups of 4. Top 2 from each group plus 8 best third-place teams advance to a 32-team knockout round. More matches, more upsets, more drama.
🇺🇸USA host cities: New York, Los Angeles, Dallas, San Francisco, Seattle, Atlanta, Boston, Kansas City, Philadelphia, Miami, Guadalupe (Texas).
🇨🇦Canada host cities: Vancouver and Toronto — Canada's only two venues.
🏆Final: July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York area).
Kids Corner ⚽
🧒 Fun Facts
The 2026 World Cup has 48 teams — that's 16 more countries than ever before! Every continent is represented. Some teams playing in Vancouver may have never qualified for a World Cup before. You might be watching history. The atmosphere inside BC Place during a match is unlike anything else — 54,000 people from all over the world, all watching the same game.
🌍
Why Three Countries?
The 2026 World Cup bid — called "United 2026" — was a joint proposal by the USA, Canada, and Mexico. It won over Morocco's bid in 2018. Sharing the tournament across North America means shorter travel for US and Canadian fans and an enormous economic boost for all three countries.
🇲🇽
Mexico Makes History
Mexico becomes the first country to host matches at three separate World Cups (1970, 1986, and 2026). The opening match is at the legendary Estadio Azteca in Mexico City on June 11 — the same day your family arrives in Vancouver.
📺
On the Ship
The group stage runs right through your cruise. Two BC Place matches fall during your sailing — Canada vs. Qatar (Jun 18, Skagway day, 3pm) and New Zealand vs. Egypt (Jun 21, last sea day, 6pm). Disney Wonder's pub and lounges will almost certainly have screens on. Watching World Cup soccer on a cruise ship in Alaska sounds like a pretty great story.
