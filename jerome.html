Quick History
Jerome boomed as a copper mining town from 1876 to 1953, once housing 15,000 people. When the mines closed, the population crashed to just 50. Artists moved in during the 1960s and rescued the town. Today it's a National Historic Landmark packed with galleries, wine bars, and a wonderfully haunted reputation.

Red Rock Country
Sedona
28 mi from Jerome · 4,350 ft · Hike early
🌡️
June Heat — Plan Smart
Sedona averages 100–108°F in late June. Start any trail by 7:30–8:00 AM. Carry 1 liter of water per mile per person. A Red Rock Pass ($12/car) is required at most trailheads. Midday = Tlaquepaque, Chapel of the Holy Cross, or a winery.
Top Trails — Go Early!
⛰️
Cathedral Rock Must
The most photographed rock formation in Arizona. A moderate scramble with iron rungs near the top — the view from the saddle between the twin spires is unforgettable.
1.5 mi round trip Moderate — some scrambling Red Rock Pass $12
Tap for details →
🔔
Bell Rock Trail
Most accessible iconic formation — a flat path leads right to the base, and you can scramble partway up the bell shape for great views. One of Sedona's four major vortex sites.
3.6 mi loop Easy to Moderate Red Rock Pass $12

Airport Mesa Loop Best Sunset
360° views of every famous Sedona formation from a single point. The viewpoint off Airport Rd requires no hiking. The full loop is 3.3 miles with sweeping panoramas throughout.
3.3 mi loop (or just the viewpoint) Easy to Moderate

Devil's Bridge
The largest natural sandstone arch in the Sedona area. The iconic photo where you stand on the arch above a dramatic drop. Very crowded on summer weekends — go at sunrise or skip.
