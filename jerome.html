Quick History
Jerome boomed as a copper mining town from 1876 to 1953, once housing 15,000 people. When the mines closed, the population crashed to 50. Artists moved in during the 1960s and rescued the town. Today it's a National Historic Landmark packed with galleries, wine bars, and a wonderfully haunted reputation.

Don't Miss
🏺
Tuzigoot National Monument Must
A 12th–13th century Sinagua hilltop pueblo with over 110 excavated rooms. One of Arizona's most impressive ancient ruins — and the Verde Valley views from the top are extraordinary.
$10 / person Daily 9am–5pm 13 mi from Jerome
Tap for details + directions →
🚂
Verde Canyon Railroad Must
A 4-hour round-trip train ride through a remote desert canyon inaccessible by road. Bald eagle habitat, towering canyon walls, ancient ruins visible from the windows. One of Arizona's great experiences.
$65–$95 / person Book ahead Departs Clarkdale · 14 mi

Arizona's most concentrated wine district — 10+ tasting rooms within walking distance along N Main St. Great food, boutiques, and a relaxed walkable vibe very different from Jerome's steep streets.

Day Trips
🏯
Montezuma Castle National Monument
A five-story cliff dwelling carved into a limestone recess 90 feet above the valley floor — one of the best-preserved pre-Columbian structures in the United States. Fast visit (30–45 min) on your way out of town.
$10 / person Daily 8am–5pm 25 mi from Jerome · I-17

🦅
Dead Horse Ranch State Park
Verde River access, a stocked fishing lagoon, excellent bird watching, and easy riverside trails. One of Arizona's most underrated state parks — peaceful contrast to Jerome's intensity.
